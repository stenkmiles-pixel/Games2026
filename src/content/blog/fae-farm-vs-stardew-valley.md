Two beloved cozy farming sims, two very different approaches. If you're torn between **Fae Farm and Stardew Valley**, here's a clear, no-nonsense comparison to help you pick the right one.

**The short answer:** Stardew Valley for unbeatable depth and value; Fae Farm for polish, easy co-op, and a gentler, magical onboarding.

## At a glance

| | Stardew Valley | Fae Farm |
|---|---|---|
| Style | 2D pixel art | Cute, modern 3D |
| Price | Budget-friendly | Full-price |
| Co-op | Up to 4 (more with mods) | Up to 4, built-in |
| Depth | Enormous | Approachable |
| Mods | Huge mod scene (PC) | None |
| Best for | Depth, value, tinkering | Polish, ease, playing together |

## Stardew Valley: the deep, all-time classic

Stardew is the genre's benchmark. Made (largely) by one developer, it's astonishingly deep: farming, fishing, mining, combat, a full town to befriend, and secrets you'll find years later. It's cheap, runs on [almost anything](/blog/best-cozy-games-low-end-pc/), and has a massive [mod community](/blog/best-stardew-valley-mods/) on PC.

The trade-off: the pixel art and dense systems can feel old-school, and the early game asks you to manage your time and energy carefully. Our [beginner's guide](/blog/stardew-valley-beginner-guide/) smooths that out.

## Fae Farm: the polished, approachable newcomer

Fae Farm (from Phoenix Labs) is brighter, gentler, and more modern. The 3D world is adorable, the magic-and-fae theme is charming, and four-player [co-op](/blog/best-cozy-coop-games/) is baked in and easy to use. It holds your hand more, with a softer difficulty curve and no harsh penalties.

The trade-off: it costs more, it's not as deep or as endlessly replayable as Stardew, and there are no mods to extend it.

## Which should you buy?

- **Buy Stardew Valley if:** you want the deepest, best-value cozy farming sim, you enjoy mastering systems, or you play on PC and love mods.
- **Buy Fae Farm if:** you want a polished, gentle, great-looking game to relax with — especially in co-op with friends or a partner who's newer to games.

Honestly? Many cozy fans end up owning both. But if it's one or the other, let your priorities — depth and value, or polish and ease — make the call.
