A lively island needs lovely neighbors. If you're wondering **how to get more villagers in Animal Crossing: New Horizons**, here's every method — plus how to make room for them.

## First, make room

New Horizons supports up to **ten villagers** at once. Before inviting anyone, you'll need an open housing plot:

- Talk to Tom Nook and choose to **sell a housing kit** / set out a plot. You'll place the plot, and it can then be filled by a new resident.
- You can have several empty plots waiting at once, ready for new arrivals.

## Ways to invite new villagers

### 1. Mystery Island tours (the main method)
Buy a **Nook Miles Ticket** from the Nook Stop terminal, then fly to a random "mystery island" from the airport. If a villager is there, talk to them and invite them to move in (you'll need an open plot). Repeat for more chances.

### 2. The campsite
Once you've built the **campsite**, visitors will occasionally stop by. Chat with them and you can invite them to live on your island.

### 3. amiibo cards
Use **amiibo cards or figures** at the campsite to invite *specific* villagers you want. This is the only reliable way to get an exact character — great for fan favorites.

### 4. Villagers who ask to move in
Occasionally a villager you meet (on a tour or via friends' islands) will be open to moving, so always say hello.

### 5. Visiting friends' islands
If a friend has a villager in **boxes** (packed up to move), you may be able to invite them to your island instead.

## Tips for a happy island

- **Raise your island rating.** A higher star rating (via décor, flowers, and tidiness) helps attract residents and unlocks features.
- **Keep a plot open** whenever you're island-hopping, or you can't invite anyone you meet.
- **Be patient with tours.** Mystery islands are random, so finding the *right* villager can take several Nook Miles Tickets — stock up first.
- **Want a specific dream villager?** amiibo cards are the fastest, surest route.
