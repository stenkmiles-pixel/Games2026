One of the quiet joys of cozy games is the music — gentle, warm, and made to keep you calm for hours. The good news: you can play these soundtracks any time, whether you're studying, working, or just unwinding. Here are the **best cozy game soundtracks** to put on loop.

## The all-time comfort picks

### Stardew Valley
ConcernedApe's soundtrack is a cozy-gaming staple — each season has its own gentle theme, and the spring/fall tracks are pure comfort. Perfect background music for focus.

### Spiritfarer
A beautiful, emotional score that's wonderful for slow, reflective work. Calming without being boring. (We love the game too — see our [review](/blog/spiritfarer-review/).)

### Animal Crossing
The hourly music is practically designed to be a pleasant, low-key backdrop. The K.K. Slider catalogue is a rabbit hole of chill tunes.

## Gentle and atmospheric

### A Short Hike
Breezy, warm, and uplifting — great for a light, happy focus session.

### Coffee Talk
Lo-fi, jazzy, rainy-night vibes. Possibly the most "study playlist" soundtrack on this list.

### Chicory: A Colorful Tale
A lovely, expressive score (with work from Lena Raine) that's both soothing and quietly inspiring.

## How to listen
Most cozy soundtracks are on **Spotify, YouTube, and Apple Music** — search the game name plus "soundtrack" or "OST." Many composers also post official playlists. Queue one up, lower the volume a touch, and let it carry your afternoon.

## Why game music works for focus
Game soundtracks are *built* to be heard for hours without distracting you — looping gently, staying in the background, and keeping a steady mood. That makes them ideal for studying, working, or [mindful downtime](/blog/why-cozy-games-good-for-stress/).
