Ask any veteran what the best long-term money crop in Stardew Valley is, and most will say the same thing: **Ancient Fruit**. Here's how to get it and turn it into a gold-printing machine.

New to farming? Start with our [beginner's guide](/blog/stardew-valley-beginner-guide/) and [best crops guide](/blog/stardew-valley-best-crops/).

## Why Ancient Fruit is so good

- It **regrows** every 7 days after the first harvest, so you plant once and harvest all season.
- It grows in **spring, summer, and fall** (and year-round in the greenhouse or on Ginger Island).
- Turned into **wine** in kegs, it sells for a fortune — especially with the Artisan profession.

Plant it in your greenhouse and you've got passive income forever.

## How to get your first Ancient Seed

The tricky part is getting started. Here are the main ways:

1. **Find an Ancient Seed artifact**, then donate it to the museum — Gunther rewards you with a **packet of Ancient Seeds** you can plant.
   - Artifacts drop from **artifact spots** (the wiggly worm patches), **digging/tilling**, **fishing treasure chests**, and **monster drops** in the mines.
2. **Seed Maker loop**: once you have even one Ancient Fruit, put it in a **Seed Maker** to get more Ancient Seeds (sometimes several). This is how you scale from one plant to a whole greenhouse.
3. **The Traveling Cart** occasionally sells Ancient Seeds or Ancient Fruit (pricey, but a shortcut).

## The money loop

1. Plant your first Ancient Seeds (greenhouse is ideal).
2. Harvest Ancient Fruit, then run some through a **Seed Maker** to multiply your seeds.
3. Fill the greenhouse with Ancient Fruit; it regrows every 7 days.
4. Put the fruit into **kegs** to make Ancient Fruit **wine** — the real money. With the **Artisan profession** (Farming level 10), it sells for even more.

Do this and you'll have a reliable five-figure income every week.
