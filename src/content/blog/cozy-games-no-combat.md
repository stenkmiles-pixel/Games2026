Not everyone wants to fight monsters to relax. For a lot of players — especially newcomers — combat is the opposite of cozy. The good news is that some of the most beloved games around have **no combat whatsoever**.

Here are the **best cozy games with no combat**, ideal if you're new to gaming or just want to switch your brain off.

## Pure, peaceful classics

### Unpacking
Unpack boxes and arrange a home across the years. There's nothing to fight, fail, or rush — just a quietly moving story told through objects.

### A Short Hike
Explore a sunny island, climb a mountain, and glide on the breeze. Warm, wholesome, and completely conflict-free.

### Dorfromantik
Place tiles to grow a serene landscape. No enemies, no timers — just calm, creative puzzling.

### Townscaper
Not even really a game — just click to build a charming seaside town. The definition of relaxing.

## Cozy life & creativity

### Animal Crossing: New Horizons
Decorate, garden, and befriend villagers on your island. The gentlest life sim there is. Great on [Switch](/blog/best-cozy-games-switch/).

### Coffee Talk
Brew drinks and listen to your customers' stories in a rainy late-night café. It's all conversation and warmth.

### PowerWash Simulator
Blast away grime with a pressure washer. Weirdly meditative, with zero stakes.

### House Flipper
Clean, renovate, and decorate houses. Satisfying, tidy, and totally combat-free.

## "Wait, doesn't that one have combat?"

A quick note on a few popular cozy games:

- **Stardew Valley** *does* have optional combat in the mines — but you can largely ignore it and focus on farming, fishing, and friendships. See our [beginner's guide](/blog/stardew-valley-beginner-guide/).
- **Spiritfarer** has no real combat, just gentle platforming and caretaking.
- **Coral Island** has light, optional dungeon-diving you can mostly skip.

If "no combat at all" is a hard requirement, stick to the top two sections above — every game there is 100% fight-free.

## Why no-combat games are great for beginners

Without combat, there's no reflexes to master and no fail states to stress about. You can learn the controls at your own pace and simply enjoy the world. That makes these games perfect first games for anyone who's said "I'm just not a gamer."
