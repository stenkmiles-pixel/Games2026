Cozy games are perfect for kids: gentle, creative, and free of the stress and violence found in many titles. Here are the **best cozy games for kids**, grouped by age and reading level so you can pick the right fit.

## Great for younger kids (little reading needed)

### Slime Rancher
Bright, bouncy, first-person ranching with adorable slimes. Easy to grasp and endlessly cheerful.

### A Short Hike
A gentle exploration game about climbing a mountain and gliding around. Simple controls, lots of charm.

### Lego Builder's Journey
A calm, wordless puzzle game with soothing music — lovely for younger players (and grown-ups).

### Untitled Goose Game
Play a mischievous goose causing gentle chaos. Funny, harmless, and a guaranteed giggle.

## Great for kids who can read

### Animal Crossing: New Horizons
A wholesome island life sim with no fail states — perfect for kids to potter around in. A [Switch](/blog/best-cozy-games-switch/) staple.

### Stardew Valley
Wonderful for slightly older kids who enjoy reading and planning. Our [beginner's guide](/blog/stardew-valley-beginner-guide/) helps them (and you) start.

### Garden Paws
Run a shop and farm as cute animals, with co-op so you can play together.

### Bugsnax
A colorful, funny adventure about catching food-creatures. Quirky and very kid-friendly.

## Lovely to play together

Many of these support [co-op](/blog/best-cozy-coop-games/), making them great for playing alongside your kids — Stardew Valley, Garden Paws, and Fae Farm all let you share the fun.

## Tips for parents

- **Check the reading level.** Games like Stardew involve reading; younger kids may prefer Slime Rancher or Lego Builder's Journey.
- **No fail states = no frustration.** Cozy games rarely punish mistakes, which keeps things stress-free for little ones.
- **Play together.** Co-op cozy games are a wonderful, calm way to share screen time as a family.
