Few cozy games have charmed players as fast as Fields of Mistria. With its gorgeous animated pixel art and warm small-town vibe, it became an instant favorite in early access. Here are our first impressions — and whether to play it now or wait.

**In short:** already one of the most delightful farming sims around, with the polish to become a genre great — just remember it's still in development.

## What is Fields of Mistria?

From developer NPC Studio, Fields of Mistria is a farming-and-life sim clearly inspired by Stardew Valley and classic Harvest Moon, wrapped in a soft, anime-influenced pixel-art style. You restore a farm, befriend (and romance) the townsfolk, mine, fish, and uncover the town's mysteries.

It's in **early access on PC (Steam)** — meaning it's playable and lovely now, but still being expanded with more story and content.

## What's wonderful

- **The art is stunning.** Easily some of the most beautiful, expressive pixel art in the genre — characters and animations ooze charm.
- **It feels great to play.** Smooth, polished, and welcoming, even in early access — an impressively gentle on-ramp.
- **Lovable characters.** The cast is endearing, and the town has a warm, inviting personality.
- **Lots of promise.** Frequent updates keep adding content, and the foundation is excellent.

## What to keep in mind

- **It's early access.** The story and some systems aren't finished — content is still being added over time.
- **If you want a "complete" game**, you may prefer to wait for the 1.0 release. If you're happy to grow with it, dive in now.

## Who should play it now?

**Play it now if:** you adore the look, love farming sims, and don't mind an in-progress game that's already a joy.

**Wait if:** you'd rather experience the full story in one go — wishlist it and watch for 1.0. See more upcoming titles in our [new cozy games to watch in 2026](/blog/new-cozy-games-2026/).

## The verdict

Even unfinished, Fields of Mistria is a treat — beautiful, charming, and brimming with potential. It's one of the brightest stars in the modern cozy farming-sim wave.

**Worth it? Yes**, if you're comfortable with early access. Otherwise, it's absolutely one to wishlist.
