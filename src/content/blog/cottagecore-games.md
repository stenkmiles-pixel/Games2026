If you love the cottagecore aesthetic — slow living, wildflowers, baking bread, and a little cottage in the countryside — there's a whole genre of games made for you. Here's the **ultimate list of cottagecore games** to romanticize your gaming life.

## The cottagecore essentials

### Stardew Valley
The ultimate cottagecore fantasy: leave the city, inherit a farm, and build a gentle rural life. Start with our [beginner's guide](/blog/stardew-valley-beginner-guide/).

### Wylde Flowers
Farming, baking, and witchcraft in a close-knit seaside village, with a heartfelt voiced story. Peak cozy cottagecore — see our [review](/blog/wylde-flowers-review/).

### Roots of Pacha
A Stone Age cottagecore life sim focused on community, farming, and discovery.

## Witchy & whimsical cottagecore

### Wytchwood
A gothic-cottagecore crafting adventure about gathering ingredients and brewing curious concoctions.

### Spiritfarer
Gather, cook, garden, and care for spirits aboard a cozy boat. Gentle and beautiful — read our [review](/blog/spiritfarer-review/).

### Garden Story
Be a little grape tending a community back to life. Wholesome and charming.

## Slow-living & nature

### Botany Manor
A serene puzzle game about a retired botanist growing rare plants in a beautiful manor.

### A Short Hike
Hike, glide, and explore a peaceful nature park. The coziest afternoon escape.

### Bear and Breakfast
Run a cozy woodland bed-and-breakfast as a well-meaning bear. Management with cottagecore charm.

## Coming soon for cottagecore fans

Keep an eye on upcoming titles like **Witchbrook** and **Tales of the Shire** — see our [new cozy games to watch in 2026](/blog/new-cozy-games-2026/).

## What makes a game "cottagecore"?

It's all about **rural calm**: nature, gardening, foraging, cooking, and a slow, self-directed pace — usually with no combat and a warm, pastoral aesthetic. If a game makes you want to bake bread and press flowers, it belongs here.
