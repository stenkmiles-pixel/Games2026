If you love the cottagecore aesthetic — slow living, wildflowers, baking bread, and a little cottage in the countryside — there's a whole genre of games made for you. These are the games about leaving the rush behind to tend a garden, forage in the woods, and live gently with the seasons. Here's the **ultimate list of cottagecore games** to romanticize your gaming life.

## The cottagecore essentials

### Stardew Valley
The ultimate cottagecore fantasy: leave a soulless city job, inherit your grandfather's overgrown farm, and slowly build a gentle rural life of crops, animals, foraging and friendship. It's deep, affordable and endlessly warm. Start with our [beginner's guide](/blog/stardew-valley-beginner-guide/).

### Fields of Mistria
A stunning pixel-art farming sim with a soft, storybook aesthetic that's pure cottagecore — flowers, festivals, mining and romance in a charming mountain village. It's one of the most beloved newcomers in the genre; see our [review](/blog/fields-of-mistria-review/).

### Wylde Flowers
Farming, baking, and witchcraft in a close-knit seaside village, wrapped in a heartfelt, fully voiced story. By day you tend your farm; by night you join a coven. Peak cosy cottagecore — read our [review](/blog/wylde-flowers-review/).

### Roots of Pacha
A Stone Age cottagecore life sim focused on community, farming and discovery. Instead of money and machines, you share inventions with your tribe — a warm, communal spin on the formula, with co-op.

## Witchy & whimsical cottagecore

### Wytchwood
A gothic-cottagecore crafting adventure about gathering ingredients from an enchanted forest and brewing curious concoctions to fulfil strange bargains. Gorgeous, storybook art and a satisfying gather-and-craft loop.

### Spiritfarer
Gather, cook, garden and care for spirits aboard a cosy boat that you build into a floating cottage. It's gentle, beautiful and quietly moving — one of our favourites, covered in full in our [review](/blog/spiritfarer-review/).

### Garden Story
Play a little grape named Concord tending a community back to life across a charming island. It's wholesome and gently adventurous, with a soft, pastoral world to restore.

## Slow-living & nature

### Botany Manor
A serene first-person puzzle game about a retired 19th-century botanist coaxing rare plants to bloom in a beautiful manor and its grounds. Quiet, clever, and deeply cottagecore.

### Bear and Breakfast
Run a cosy woodland bed-and-breakfast as a well-meaning bear — decorating rooms, welcoming guests and fixing up the forest. Light management with bags of pastoral charm and humour.

### A Short Hike
Hike, glide, and explore a peaceful nature park at your own pace. It's the coziest little afternoon escape, all rolling hills, friendly characters and fresh air.

## What makes a game "cottagecore"?

It's all about **rural calm**: nature, gardening, foraging, cooking, and a slow, self-directed pace — usually with no combat and a warm, pastoral aesthetic. If a game makes you want to bake bread and press flowers, it belongs here.
