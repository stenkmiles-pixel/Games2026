The PS5's reputation is built on cinematic blockbusters, but it has quietly become one of the best places to play cozy games. Fast loading means no waiting between relaxing sessions, the DualSense's soft haptics suit slow, tactile games, and these games look wonderful on a big screen at the end of a long day.

Below are the **best cozy games on PS5** — the ones we keep coming back to when we want to unwind rather than be challenged. We've grouped them from must-play essentials to lovely extras, with a note on what makes each one cozy and who it's for.

## The cozy essentials

### Stardew Valley
The cornerstone of the entire genre, and it runs flawlessly on PS5. You inherit a run-down farm and slowly rebuild it — planting crops, raising animals, fishing, mining, and befriending (or marrying) the townsfolk. It's astonishingly deep for the price, endlessly replayable, and the rare game you can sink 200 hours into without ever feeling stressed. New to it? Start with our [beginner's guide](/blog/stardew-valley-beginner-guide/).

### Coral Island
If you want a game that *shows off* the PS5, this is it. Coral Island is a gorgeous 3D farming sim set on a tropical island, with a twist the classics don't have: scuba diving and restoring a coral reef alongside your farm. It's warm, generous, and welcoming, with a large cast to befriend. Our [Coral Island review](/blog/coral-island-review/) has the full picture.

### Disney Dreamlight Valley
A life sim built around Disney and Pixar characters, fully featured on PS5. You farm, cook, decorate and complete gentle quests while befriending the likes of Mickey, Moana and WALL-E. The decorating is wonderfully deep and there's no combat — just charm. See [is it worth it?](/blog/disney-dreamlight-valley-worth-it/) for the honest take on its one catch (microtransactions).

### Spiritfarer
A "cozy management game about dying," which sounds heavy but is one of the most beautiful, moving experiences here. You play a ferrymaster guiding spirits to the afterlife — building your boat, cooking their favourite meals, and saying goodbye. Hand-drawn, gentle, and quietly profound. We cover it in full in our [Spiritfarer review](/blog/spiritfarer-review/).

## More gentle PS5 picks

### Fae Farm
A polished, approachable farming-and-magic sim with drop-in co-op for up to four friends. It's friendlier and more guided than Stardew, which makes it a great pick for newcomers or couples. Compare the two in [Fae Farm vs Stardew Valley](/blog/fae-farm-vs-stardew-valley/).

### Story of Seasons: A Wonderful Life
A remake of the beloved farming-and-family classic. It's slower and more relaxed than modern sims — you marry, raise a child, and watch a whole life unfold on the farm over the years. Pure, unhurried comfort.

### Unpacking
A zen puzzle game about pulling belongings out of boxes and finding a place for them in a new home. There's no timer and no fail state — just the quiet satisfaction of arranging a life, with a touching story told entirely through the objects you place. The DualSense makes every item feel tactile.

### PowerWash Simulator
Improbably soothing: you blast dirt off houses, cars and playgrounds with a power washer, and that's the whole game. The slow reveal of a clean surface is weirdly addictive and totally stakes-free — ideal for [decompressing after work](/blog/best-relaxing-games-after-work/).

### Cozy Grove
A hand-drawn life sim played in short daily bursts. You're a Spirit Scout on a haunted-but-friendly island, helping gentle ghost bears and slowly bringing colour back to the world. It's designed to be played a little each day, Animal Crossing style.

### A Little to the Left
A cozy puzzle game about tidying — sorting drawers, lining up books and arranging shelves "just so," while a mischievous cat undoes your work. Deeply satisfying for anyone who finds order relaxing. Read our [full review](/blog/a-little-to-the-left-review/).

### Dave the Diver
A little more involved than the rest, but hard to leave off: by day you explore a beautiful, ever-changing ocean, and by night you run a sushi restaurant. It's charming, funny, and surprisingly moreish — a perfect "one more dive" game.

## How we picked these

Every game here is genuinely available and runs well on PS5, and every one fits the cozy brief: low stress, no harsh fail states, and a warm world you play to relax in. We've leaned toward games we've spent real time with, and we note where a pick is more involved (like Dave the Diver) so you know what you're getting.

## A quick note on Animal Crossing

One of the most-asked questions: **is Animal Crossing on PS5?** No — Animal Crossing is a Nintendo exclusive and only available on Switch. If that daily island-life vibe is what you want on PlayStation, start with **Disney Dreamlight Valley**, **Cozy Grove**, or **Dinkum**, or see our full list of [games like Animal Crossing](/blog/cozy-games-like-animal-crossing/).
