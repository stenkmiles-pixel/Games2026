There's something deeply soothing about fishing in games — the quiet water, the gentle wait, the little thrill of a catch. It's the perfect low-stakes loop: cast, relax, collect, repeat. Here are the **best cozy fishing games**, whether you want peaceful solo casts, a heartfelt story, or a silly fishing hangout with friends.

## Games where fishing is the main event

### WEBFISHING
A delightfully chill multiplayer game where you fish in a cozy lake, decorate your character, strum a guitar, and chat with strangers who are all just there to vibe. There's no pressure and no goal beyond filling your fish journal — it became a surprise hit precisely because it's one of the most relaxing online hangouts around. Best enjoyed with the in-game radio on.

### Moonglow Bay
A slice-of-life fishing RPG with a heartfelt story and a charming, blocky voxel art style. You take over a struggling fishing business in a 1980s coastal town, learning new techniques, cooking your catch, and slowly healing a community. Fishing meets cozy narrative, and it has couch co-op if you want a partner on the boat.

### Dave the Diver
Part relaxing ocean explorer, part sushi-restaurant manager: by day you dive a beautiful, ever-changing reef catching fish, and by night you serve them in your restaurant. It's more involved than most games here, but it's endlessly charming, funny, and very hard to put down — a brilliant "one more dive" loop.

### Webbed and other gentle catch-em-ups
If you love the *collecting* side of fishing, the same satisfaction shows up across the cozy genre — filling a journal, completing a set, and discovering a rare catch. That collector's itch is exactly what the sims below scratch so well.

## Great fishing inside cozy sims

### Stardew Valley
Fishing is one of the best parts of Stardew Valley — meditative on rainy days, a great early money-maker, and genuinely skilful once you get the rhythm of the little catch minigame. There are dozens of fish across seasons, weather and locations to discover. See our [money guide](/blog/stardew-valley-make-money-fast/) for how to turn the hobby into gold.

### Coral Island
Beautiful fishing *and* free diving, all in service of restoring an ocean reef. Casting from the pier at sunset is gorgeous, and the underwater clean-up gives your fishing a real purpose. Our [Coral Island review](/blog/coral-island-review/) has the full picture.

### Spiritfarer
Casting a line off the back of your boat as the sun sets is peak cozy, and it's just one of many gentle activities in this beautiful management game about caring for spirits. One of our [reviewed](/blog/spiritfarer-review/) favourites for a reason.

### Palia
A free cozy MMO where fishing sits alongside farming, cooking and decorating in a relaxed open world. It's a no-cost way to enjoy a gentle fishing loop with other people pottering around nearby. We weigh it up in [is Palia worth playing?](/blog/is-palia-worth-playing-2026/)

### Animal Crossing: New Horizons
The comfort-food classic: catch fish (and bugs) to fill your museum and earn Bells, at your own pace, with no pressure at all. A staple of any cozy [Switch](/blog/best-cozy-games-switch/) library.

## What makes fishing "cozy"

The best cozy fishing isn't twitchy or punishing — it's about the calm rhythm of casting, waiting, and reeling in something new for your collection. No leaderboards, no harsh timers, no failure: just you, the water, and the quiet satisfaction of a full journal. That's why fishing fits the cozy genre so naturally, whether it's the whole game or a single peaceful corner of a bigger world.
