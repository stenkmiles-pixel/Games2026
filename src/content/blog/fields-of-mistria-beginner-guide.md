Fields of Mistria is the gorgeous, anime-inspired farming sim that everyone fell for in Early Access — a cozy mix of Stardew Valley's depth and Harvest Moon's charm. You arrive in a sleepy valley town recovering from an earthquake, inherit a farm, and slowly help Mistria bloom again. It's welcoming and low-pressure, but there's a lot to learn. Here are the **beginner tips** that make your first week click.

If you're still deciding whether it's for you, read our [Fields of Mistria review](/blog/fields-of-mistria-review/) first.

## The basics

Mistria runs on the rhythms you'd expect from the genre, all at your own pace:

- **Farming** — clear your plot, till, plant, and water crops each morning
- **Mining** — dig through the mines for ore, gems, and the occasional gentle monster
- **Fishing** — at the river, ponds, and coast
- **Foraging** — pick wild plants, flowers, and mushrooms as the seasons change
- **The Museum** — donate finds to rebuild the town's collection
- **Townsfolk** — talk to and befriend (or romance) the residents

Each activity levels up over time, unlocking better tools, recipes, and rewards.

## 10 beginner tips

### 1. Water crops first, every single morning
Crops won't grow on days you forget to water them. Make the watering can your first stop each day, then plan everything else around it. Early on, plant only as much as you can comfortably water by hand.

### 2. Don't overplant in week one
It's tempting to fill your whole field, but watering eats your energy and your morning. Start with a modest patch, sell that first harvest, and expand once you can afford sprinklers and upgrades.

### 3. Forage everything you walk past
Wild flowers, herbs, and mushrooms are free money and free gifts. Grab everything on your way to and from town — foraging levels up just by doing it, and those items are perfect for befriending villagers.

### 4. Talk to everyone, every day
A quick daily chat with each resident slowly builds friendship — no gifts required. Friendship unlocks story scenes, recipes, and rewards, so a lap around town each morning pays off fast.

### 5. Learn the mine's gentle rhythm
The mines give you ore for tool upgrades and gems for gifts and donations. Combat is light and forgiving, but watch your energy and the clock — head back up before you're exhausted or it gets late.

### 6. Manage your energy and bedtime
Most actions cost energy, and staying up too late means a groggy, low-energy next day. Eat foraged or cooked food to refill energy, and try to be in bed before midnight.

### 7. Donate to the Museum as you go
Drop off the first of every mineral, fish, and artifact you find. Completing collections rewards you and is one of the most satisfying long-term goals in the game.

### 8. Upgrade your tools early
Take ore to the blacksmith to upgrade your watering can, hoe, and pickaxe. Better tools cover more tiles and save huge amounts of energy — it's the single biggest early quality-of-life jump.

### 9. Follow the quests and "Sources of Stone" jobs
Town quests are your roadmap — they introduce systems, point you toward what to gather, and give helpful rewards. When you're not sure what to do next, check your tasks.

### 10. Let yourself play slowly
Mistria isn't a race. It updates regularly in Early Access and rewards a calm, curious pace far more than a min-maxed sprint. Some days, just fishing at sunset is the whole point.
