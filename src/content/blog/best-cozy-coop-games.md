Cozy games are lovely on your own — but sharing one with a friend or partner is something special. Here are the **best cozy co-op games** to relax with together, whether you're on the same couch or across the world.

## The co-op classics

### Stardew Valley
The gold standard for cozy co-op. Up to four (and more with mods) can share a farm — split the chores, fish together, and watch your valley grow. New players should read our [beginner's guide](/blog/stardew-valley-beginner-guide/) first.

### Fae Farm
Built with co-op in mind — up to four players can farm, explore, and decorate together. Polished and approachable. See how it compares in [Fae Farm vs Stardew Valley](/blog/fae-farm-vs-stardew-valley/).

### Palia
A free-to-play cozy MMO where you'll naturally bump into other players while farming, fishing, and building. Great for playing alongside friends with zero pressure.

## Build and farm together

### Sun Haven
A big pixel-art farming RPG with online co-op — share a farm and tackle its fantasy world as a team.

### Dinkum
An Australian-outback life sim with multiplayer, so you can develop your island together.

### Garden Paws
Run a shop and farm as adorable animal characters, with drop-in co-op for friends.

## Cozy management with friends

### Travellers Rest
Run a medieval tavern together — brew, cook, and serve guests as a team. A lovely shared loop.

### Wholesome party games
For shorter sessions, gentle party-style games are a great couch option when you want something quick and giggly rather than a long campaign.

## Tips for great cozy co-op

- **Check the co-op type.** Some games are online-only, some local-only, some both — confirm before buying.
- **Agree on goals.** Decide together whether you're min-maxing or just vibing; it keeps a shared save happy.
- **Match your schedules.** Games like Stardew that share one world are best when you can play together regularly.
