You don't need an expensive gaming PC to enjoy the cozy genre. In fact, many of the best cozy games are deliberately lightweight — they'll happily run on an old laptop, a work machine, or integrated graphics.

Here are the **best cozy games for a low-end PC**, all of which run well on modest hardware.

## Runs on almost anything

### Stardew Valley
Tiny system requirements, enormous game. Stardew runs on practically any computer made in the last decade, including very old laptops.

### Unpacking
A 2D zen puzzle about unpacking boxes. Light on resources, heavy on charm.

### A Short Hike
Stylised low-poly visuals mean it runs smoothly on weak hardware while still looking lovely.

### Dorfromantik
A calm tile-builder that's as light as it is relaxing — perfect for a low-spec machine.

## Cozy and light

### Littlewood
A pixel-art town-builder with no stamina system and very low requirements.

### Garden Story
Be a grape. Tend a community. Adorable, gentle, and easy to run.

### Spiritfarer
A little more demanding than the others here, but still runs on most modest laptops at lower settings.

### Coffee Talk
A visual-novel café where you brew drinks and listen to patrons. Essentially text and lovely pixel art — runs anywhere.

## Strategy & sim

### Mini Motorways
A minimalist road-drawing puzzle. Clean, low-spec, and quietly addictive.

### Slime Rancher
Slightly heavier 3D, but well-optimized — playable on integrated graphics at lower settings.

## How to check if a game will run

- **Look up the minimum requirements** on the Steam page and compare them to your laptop's specs (CPU, RAM, and whether you have a dedicated GPU).
- **Use the demo.** Many cozy games offer a free Steam demo — the surest way to test performance before buying.
- **Drop the resolution and settings.** Most of these games look great even at lower settings.
