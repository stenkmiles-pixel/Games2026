Palia is a free-to-play cozy MMO where you gather, craft, decorate, and befriend a whole town alongside other players. It's wonderfully relaxed — but there's a lot to take in at first. Here are the **beginner tips** to start strong.

## The basics

Palia is built around **gathering skills** rather than combat-heavy progression. Your core activities are:

- **Foraging** — picking plants and flowers
- **Gardening** — growing crops on your plot
- **Fishing** — at lakes and the coast
- **Mining** — for ore and gems
- **Bug catching** and **hunting** — for materials and recipes

Doing these levels up the matching skill, unlocking better tools and recipes.

## Beginner tips

### 1. Gather everything early
In your first hours, pick up every plant, ore, and bug you see. Materials feed almost every recipe and quest, and gathering levels your skills naturally.

### 2. Don't rush — it's a marathon
Palia is designed to be played a little at a time. There's no pressure to "finish," so explore and gather at your own pace.

### 3. Talk to the villagers
Befriending townsfolk unlocks quests, story, and rewards. Chatting and giving gifts raises friendship — a core part of the cozy loop.

### 4. Set up your housing plot
Your plot is your home base for crafting stations, a garden, and decorating. Place crafting stations early so you can process materials.

### 5. Follow the main quests
The story quests are your roadmap — they introduce each system and unlock new areas and tools. When unsure what to do, check your quest log.

### 6. Play with (or near) others
It's an MMO, so you'll see other players gathering nearby. Group activities and community hunts are a fun, low-pressure way to earn materials.

## Why Palia is great for beginners

It's **free**, has **no combat pressure**, and lets you focus purely on the cozy stuff — gathering, growing, and decorating. That makes it one of the easiest ways to try the cozy-MMO life with nothing to lose.
