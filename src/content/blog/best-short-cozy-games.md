Not every cozy game has to swallow 100 hours of your life. Sometimes you want something you can start *and finish* — a small, perfect gem that respects your time. Here are the **best short cozy games** you can wrap up in a weekend, or even a single evening, with how long each one takes.

## Finish in an evening (1–3 hours)

### A Short Hike
Climb a mountain, glide on the breeze, and befriend some critters along the way. It's a couple of hours of pure, gentle warmth — the ultimate "I have one free evening" game, and impossible not to smile at.

### Florence
A wordless, gorgeous interactive story about first love, told in beautiful little vignettes and mini-games. It takes under an hour and lingers far longer — proof that short can be deeply moving.

### A Little to the Left
Bite-sized puzzles about tidying everyday objects into satisfying order. Play a handful, feel calmer, and stop any time — there's no pressure to binge. Read our [full review](/blog/a-little-to-the-left-review/).

## Finish in a weekend (3–8 hours)

### Unpacking
Unpack boxes across the years of someone's life and quietly uncover their whole story through the objects you place. It's satisfying from start to finish, and not a single minute too long.

### Donut County
A funny, charming game where you swallow an entire town with a hole in the ground that grows as it goes. Light, breezy and complete — a perfect palate cleanser.

### Chicory: A Colorful Tale
A bit longer than the rest, but a wonderfully gentle adventure about painting colour back into a black-and-white world, with a surprisingly heartfelt story about creativity and self-doubt.

## Cosy and complete

### Coffee Talk
You can finish the main story in a weekend, savouring each customer's tale over a warm drink in a rainy, lo-fi café. All atmosphere, no stress.

### Tiny Glade
Not a story at all, but a perfect short session: build a pretty storybook castle, admire it, and start a new one. See our [review](/blog/tiny-glade-review/) — it's the coziest way to spend a quiet hour.

## Why short cozy games are great

A short game respects your time and leaves you wanting more in the best way. They're perfect between bigger games, ideal when a sprawling farming sim feels like too much commitment, and easy on your wallet too.
