Not every cozy game has to swallow 100 hours of your life. Sometimes you want something you can start *and finish* — a small, perfect gem. Here are the **best short cozy games** you can wrap up in a weekend, or even a single evening.

## Finish in an evening (1–3 hours)

### A Short Hike
Climb a mountain, glide on the breeze, befriend some critters. A couple of hours of pure warmth — the ultimate "I have one free evening" game.

### Florence
A wordless, gorgeous story about first love, told in interactive vignettes. Beautiful and brief.

### A Little to the Left
Bite-sized puzzles about tidying everyday objects. Play a few, feel calmer, stop any time.

## Finish in a weekend (3–8 hours)

### Unpacking
Unpack boxes across the years and uncover a whole life. Satisfying start to finish, and not a minute too long.

### Donut County
A funny, charming game where you swallow a town with a hole in the ground. Light, breezy, complete.

### Chicory: A Colorful Tale
A bit longer, but a wonderfully gentle adventure about painting color back into the world.

## Cozy and complete

### Coffee Talk
You can finish the main story in a weekend, savoring each customer's tale over a warm drink.

### Spiritfarer (the exception)
Longer than the rest, but worth a mention — if you *do* want something meatier and moving, our [Spiritfarer review](/blog/spiritfarer-review/) makes the case.

## Why short cozy games are great

A short game respects your time and leaves you wanting more in the best way. They're perfect between bigger games, or when a sprawling farming sim feels like too much commitment.
