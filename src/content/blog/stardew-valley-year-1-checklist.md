Your first year in Stardew Valley sets the tone for everything after. You don't have to do it all — but a few priorities each season pay off hugely. Here's a relaxed **Year 1 checklist** to keep you on track without turning a cozy game into a chore.

New to the valley? Start with our [beginner's guide](/blog/stardew-valley-beginner-guide/).

## Spring (Year 1)
- **Plant crops on day one** — parsnips first, then reinvest. See the [best crops guide](/blog/stardew-valley-best-crops/).
- **Buy Strawberry seeds at the Egg Festival** (day 13) and plant them — top spring earner.
- **Meet everyone in town** and start learning birthdays ([gift guide](/blog/stardew-valley-gift-guide/)).
- **Start the [Community Center](/blog/stardew-valley-community-center-guide/) bundles** — donate spring crops, foraged items, and fish.
- **Upgrade your watering can or pickaxe** when you can spare the tool for two days.

## Summer (Year 1)
- **Go big on Blueberries** (multi-harvest = steady income). Add **kegs** and start making wine.
- **Buy a coop**, then a barn — animals + artisan goods (mayo, cheese) compound your income.
- **Push into the Mines** for ore and the metals you need for upgrades.
- **Fish on rainy days** — great money and Community Center bundle progress.

## Fall (Year 1)
- **Cranberries + Pumpkins** for income; keep the kegs running.
- **Aim to finish a few Community Center rooms** — the **greenhouse** reward is a game-changer ([how to unlock it](/blog/stardew-valley-greenhouse/)).
- **Hit Friendship milestones** with anyone you're interested in befriending or romancing.
- **Catch the Traveling Cart** on Fridays/Sundays for rare seeds (maybe even [Ancient Fruit](/blog/stardew-valley-ancient-fruit/)).

## Winter (Year 1)
- Nothing grows outside — **mine deep, fish, and prep fields** for a huge Spring.
- **Finish tool upgrades** and stock up on resources.
- **Complete more bundles** with stored items and the money (Vault) bundles.

## Don't stress the "perfect" year
You won't do everything in Year 1 — and that's fine. Stardew has no real fail state. Hit a few of these each season and you'll start Year 2 rich, well-equipped, and with friends all over town.
